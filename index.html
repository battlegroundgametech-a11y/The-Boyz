Supes vs Testosterone

In The Boys, superheroes are not shown as perfect saviors. They are celebrities, weapons, products, and political symbols controlled by Vought. The show explores what happens when extreme power mixes with ego, aggression, fame, insecurity, and corporate protection.

About The Show

The Boys is a dark superhero satire set in a world where superheroes are owned and managed by a powerful corporation called Vought International. These heroes, known as “supes,” are marketed as noble protectors, but many of them are corrupt, violent, selfish, and protected by money, media, politics, and public relations.

The story follows a group of ordinary humans called The Boys, who fight against corrupt supes and expose the truth behind Vought’s empire. The show mixes action, brutal violence, dark comedy, political satire, emotional drama, and social commentary.

About The Supes

Homelander

Homelander is the leader of The Seven and Vought’s most powerful public symbol. He presents himself as patriotic, heroic, and perfect, but underneath he is unstable, narcissistic, cruel, and obsessed with control.

Queen Maeve

Queen Maeve is one of the most experienced members of The Seven. She has become cynical after years of corruption, but still carries courage, guilt, and a hidden desire to do the right thing.

A-Train

A-Train is the fastest man in the world. His story shows the pressure of fame, addiction, insecurity, competition, and how Vought treats superheroes like disposable products.

The Deep

The Deep has aquatic powers and can communicate with sea creatures. He is insecure, desperate for approval, and often used to expose the hypocrisy of celebrity culture.

Starlight

Starlight begins as an idealistic hero who wants to make the world better. After joining The Seven, she discovers Vought’s corruption and slowly becomes one of the few supes willing to resist it.

Black Noir

Black Noir is silent, mysterious, deadly, and extremely loyal to Vought. His quiet nature hides a tragic past and a deep connection to the darker history of superheroes.

About The Boys

Billy Butcher

Billy Butcher is the ruthless leader of The Boys. Fueled by hatred for supes, especially Homelander, he is willing to manipulate, fight, and sacrifice almost anything to destroy Vought.

Hughie Campbell

Hughie is an ordinary man pulled into the war against supes after a personal tragedy. He often represents the emotional heart of the team and struggles with revenge, fear, and morality.

Mother’s Milk

Mother’s Milk, also known as MM, is disciplined, responsible, and loyal. He brings structure and a strong moral compass to The Boys while dealing with his own painful history with Vought.

Frenchie

Frenchie is a creative weapons expert with a criminal past. He is chaotic, emotional, intelligent, and deeply loyal, especially toward Kimiko.

Kimiko

Kimiko is powerful, deadly, and deeply traumatized. Though she rarely speaks, she is one of the most expressive members of the group and forms a strong bond with Frenchie.

Note About Compound V

Compound V is the mysterious substance responsible for creating most superhuman abilities in the show. It represents artificial power manufactured and controlled by corporations. Instead of heroism being natural or noble, Compound V reveals how Vought turns power into a product, a weapon, and a business.
